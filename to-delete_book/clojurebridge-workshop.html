Saturday Workshop

ClojureBridge Workshop Overview

Thank you for joining us for this all day workshop today. Together with your coaches you will discover the basics of Clojure in the morning and then work together to create one or more projects together in the afternoon.

The audience for these workshops is diverse, so we provide a range examples and projects to help you do something you can relate too. This does of course mean that there is more content than anyone can cover in once day.

Info

You should have a Clojure environment set up from our event on Friday evening, if you missed this then please get in touch with an organiser straight away.

Outline for the morning

The morning covers the language syntax of Clojure, so you can speak Clojure to your computer, along with some of the core concepts of thinking functionally.

  • Simple values (numbers, characters, strings)
  • Calling functions in Clojure
  • Collections of Values
  • Functions as arguments
  • Control flow (list comprehension, for/when)
  • Iteration (loop/recur, fn/recur, filter, predicates)
  • map & reduce
  • naming
  • defining functions

Outline for the afternoon

There are many projects to choose from which you can work together in pairs or as a group.

Some projects have detailed steps you can simply follow along, whist others encourage you to explore your own solutions. As there are many ways to do things, there is no right or wrong answers, just opportunities to think and learn.

Projects currently being sourced from the community and organised

Guided projects

  • Map Reduce sandwich (incorporate into main course material)
  • Celebrity Name Smash - string manipulation: subs count, take, str, lazy sequence, apply, reduce
  • Clacks messages - simple encoding & decoding of messages, thinking about data structures: maps (key value pairs), get, map reduce (extension of the map reduce sandwich)
